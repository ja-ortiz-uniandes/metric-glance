Metric Glance test page

Load the extension, then reload this page. Converted values get a dotted underline.

1. Units should convert

The trail is 12 miles long, climbs 1,200 feet, and the sign is 18 inches wide. She ran it in shoes weighing 11 oz, carrying 5 lbs of water at 75 °F, driving 60 mph to get there. The lot covers 2.5 acres and the tank holds 12 gallons. A board 5 1/2 inches thick. He weighs 13 stone.

1c. Units inside hyperlinks / split across elements

A meter is 39.37 inches, the trail is 5 miles long, and the limit is 60 mph.

These should convert even though the unit word is a link. The converted value uses a dashed blue underline (it had hidden formatting). Hover/tap it: the panel shows the original text in a code box with the link intact. "Mark as incorrect" restores the original link.

1b. Correctness + ambiguous units (hover/tap to see rate and options)

The stone of 14 pounds (6.35029318 kg) for body weight; a barrel of 196 pounds. He owes 14 pounds for 2 gallons of cider and 3 pints of milk.

14 pounds should read 6.35 kg, 196 pounds should read 88.9 kg. Hover/tap any of them: the panel shows the rate, and for ambiguous ones (pounds, gallons, pints) lets you switch interpretation, e.g. mark "14 pounds" as British money so it stops converting.

2. Prices round UP to the next whole amount (cents threshold)

The laptop is $1,299.99, the mouse is $24.99, a snack is $1.95, something is $2.50, and another is $2.01. A round price: $500.00.

At the default 50¢: $1,299.99→$1,300, $24.99→$25, $1.95→$2; but $2.50, $2.01 and $500.00 are left alone. Raise the threshold toward 99¢ in Preferences and more of them round up ($2.01→$3 only at 99¢). Reload after changing it.

3. These should NOT convert (context traps)

He lives on Five Mile Road and shops at the Quarter Pounder stand. The meeting is at 10 min past. We met on June 1st. Section 3 in. the manual.

If any of these get underlined, that's a false positive to test the correction on.

4. Inside <code> should be ignored

const distance = 26.2 miles; const price = $9,999.99;

3a2. Dimension lists & prefixed volume units

Item dimensions: 4 x 3 x 1 in (all three convert). A rug 5 by 3 feet. A can of 8 fl oz, ≡ 1 imp fl oz.

Select any number and press Ctrl+Alt+M (or right-click → Convert selection to metric → More units…) to open the searchable unit picker: categories, search, all three fl oz, energy, pressure, esoteric units.

3b. Cubic units

The imperial gallon is ≈277.42 cu in, a box is 2 ft³, and 35 in³ of resin.

cu in / in³ → cm³ or L; cu ft / ft³ → m³ or L.

4b. Split price across elements (Amazon-style)

$12.77$1277 ← should round to $13 even though it's split and has no visible decimal point.
